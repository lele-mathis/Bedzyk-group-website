In Situ XSW Studies of the Adsorption of
Cations and Polynucleotide Polyions to Charged Surfaces

    We use long-period X-ray standing waves (XSW) for directly observing the condensation of polynucleotides and their electrolyte counterions at liquid / solid interfaces. The XSW generated by Bragg diffraction from a Si/Mo multilayer substrate (with period d = 3 to 20 nm) is used to measure the distinct distribution profiles of the polyanions and simple cations along the surface normal direction with sub-nanometer resolution. The 1D spatial sensitivity of this approach is enhanced by observing the XSW induced fluorescence modulations over multiple-orders of Bragg peaks.

    We recently studied the interesting case of divalent cation driven adsorption of anionic polynucleotides to anionic surfaces by exposing a hydroxyl-terminated silica surface to an aqueous solution with ZnCl2 and mercurated poly-uridylic acid (a synthetic RNA molecule). 1 , 2 The in situ long-period XSW measurements are used to follow the evolution of both the Zn and Hg distribution profiles during the adsorption process. Our theory collaborators, Prof. Olvera�s group , explain the conditions and physical mechanisms that govern the observed divalent cation adsorption and subsequent polynucleotide adsorption to an anionic surface by using a thermodynamic model that incorporates non-linear electrostatic effects.
